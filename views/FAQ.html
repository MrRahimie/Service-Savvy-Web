Frequently Asked Questions

What is freelancing?

Freelancing means working as an independent business rather than as an employee of a company. Freelancers are self-employed and often hired for short-term work, managing their own taxes, insurances, and retirement plans.

Do freelancers need to register a company?

It depends on the local laws and the scale of your freelancing business. In some cases, registering a company can provide legal benefits and tax advantages.

How do freelancers get paid?

Freelancers typically get paid project by project, either through direct payments like bank transfers or online platforms like PayPal. They may also use invoicing systems to bill their clients.

What is the biggest challenge faced by freelancers?

One of the biggest challenges is finding consistent work and managing cash flow. Freelancers must also handle all aspects of their business, including marketing, client relations, and finances.
